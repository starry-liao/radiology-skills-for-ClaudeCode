# Radiology house style

> Confirm specifics against the current _Radiology_ author instructions; conventions below
> are durable.

## Voice & tense
- **Active voice is acceptable and encouraged** for clarity (_Radiology_ does not mandate
  passive); use whichever is clearer. "We trained the model" is fine.
- **Tense**: Methods and Results in **past** tense ("314 patients were included," "AUC was
  0.88"); established knowledge in present; what figures show in present ("Figure 2 shows").

## Spelling & terminology (American English)
- color, tumor, fetal, esophagus, catheterization, analyze, hematoma, edema, gray (US).
- "radiologist," "reader"; specify experience ("with 8 years' experience").
- Use SI/clinical units consistently; space between number and unit (5 mm, 3 T, 1.5 T;
  exception: 90° and %). Use "T" for tesla.

## Abbreviations
- Define at **first use** in abstract and again in main text; avoid in the Title and in **Key
  Results**; don't over-abbreviate (a rarely-repeated term stays spelled out).
- Standard ones (MRI, CT, AUC, ROC) still defined per journal rule.

## Numbers
- Generally spell out numbers beginning a sentence (or recast); use numerals with units and
  for measurements/stats. Be consistent.
- Add the **n** behind percentages: "65% (204 of 314)."

## Concision (cut these)
- "It is worth noting that," "It should be emphasized," "In order to" (→ "to"), "a total
  of," "very/quite," "novel/cutting-edge," "robust" as filler.
- Nominalizations → verbs ("performed an evaluation of" → "evaluated").

## Structure-level
- One idea per sentence; one message per paragraph (first sentence states it).
- Keep each claim next to its evidence; don't separate a number from its CI.
