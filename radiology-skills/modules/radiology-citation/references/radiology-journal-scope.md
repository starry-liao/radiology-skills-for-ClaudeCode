# Imaging-journal scope & ranking

## When to scope to imaging journals
- Claims about **imaging findings, diagnostic performance, imaging technique, or radiology
  practice** → prefer imaging journals.
- Claims about **biology, genomics, ML methods, statistics** → broaden (Nature/Cell family,
  Bioinformatics, JMLR, NeurIPS/MICCAI, statistics journals). Radiogenomics usually needs
  **both**.

## Core imaging venues (illustrative, not exhaustive)
- **General radiology**: _Radiology_, _Radiology: Artificial Intelligence_, _Radiology:
  Imaging Cancer_, _Radiology: Cardiothoracic Imaging_, _RadioGraphics_, _AJR_, _European
  Radiology_, _Insights into Imaging_, _JACR_, _European Journal of Radiology_,
  _Investigative Radiology_, _Korean Journal of Radiology_.
- **Subspecialty**: _AJNR_ (neuro), _JCMR_ / _Radiology: Cardiothoracic_ (cardiac),
  _Abdominal Radiology_, _Skeletal Radiology_, _Neuroradiology_, _JMRI_.
- **Imaging-AI / methods crossover**: _Medical Image Analysis_, _IEEE TMI_, _MICCAI_
  proceedings, _Radiology: AI_.
- **Physics/quantitative**: _Medical Physics_, _Physics in Medicine & Biology_, _MRM_.

## Ranking heuristic (when several candidates support a claim)
1. Directly on-topic + strong methodological support, in a high-visibility imaging venue.
2. Primary source over review (cite the original finding); add a review for background.
3. Recent + well-cited over old, unless citing a seminal/originating paper (RQS, IBSI,
   CLAIM, STARD, TRIPOD — cite the canonical methods paper).
4. Avoid over-citing the author's own work or a single group.

## Methods-paper canonical citations (cite the source, verified)
CLAIM, TRIPOD+AI, STARD, PRISMA-DTA, QUADAS-2, RQS/RQS 2.0, CLEAR, METRICS, IBSI — when the
manuscript uses one of these, cite its originating paper (verify DOI via radiology-search).
