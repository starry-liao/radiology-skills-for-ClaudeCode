# Venue publication patterns (verify current scope live)

Orientation only — match the paper to the pattern, then **confirm the venue's current aims and
recent comparable papers** with `radiology-search` before committing. Do not select on impact
factor.

## Tiers by what they reward

| Venue | Pattern it rewards | Bar it enforces |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature Medicine** | Clinically transformative, often prospective/multi-cohort imaging AI | Strong external/prospective evidence; clinical readiness; fairness |
| **Lancet Digital Health / Lancet Oncology** | Clinical impact + generalisability | Multi-center, external or prospective validation; utility framing |
| **Radiology / Radiology: AI** | Rigorous, clinically framed imaging-AI/radiomics | External validation, calibration + DCA, reader context, EQUATOR compliance |
| **Nature Communications** | Methodological novelty + broad significance | Solid validation; convincing increment |
| **Cell Reports Medicine** | Translational + biological/clinical insight | Validation + mechanism or strong clinical relevance |
| **npj Digital Medicine / npj Precision Oncology** | Digital-health / precision-oncology angle | Validation + translational/biological relevance; open science |
| **eClinicalMedicine / eBioMedicine** | Clinically useful, translational | Clear question; adequate validation |
| **Strong specialty (e.g. EurRadiol, JNM, Med Image Anal for methods)** | Specialty/methods depth | Field-appropriate rigor; reproducibility |

## Tier-deciding signals (what moves a paper up)

- **External / prospective validation** present and honest.
- **Multi-center** with per-center results and harmonisation.
- **Reader study / clinical utility** (with vs without AI; net benefit).
- **Calibration** reported, not just discrimination.
- **Adequate sample size / events** for the claim.
- **Clear, defensible novelty** and biological grounding where relevant.
- **Reporting compliance** (CLAIM/CLEAR/TRIPOD+AI/IBSI).

## Tier-capping signals (what pulls it down)

- Single-center, single-scanner, retrospective only.
- Internal validation dressed as external.
- AUC only; no calibration/utility.
- Small n / few events; artificial 1:1 prevalence.
- Incremental method with no clinical or biological payoff.

## Honest mapping rule

If the paper has no external validation and is single-center retrospective, top clinical venues
(Nature Medicine, Lancet DH) are not a realistic target however interesting the topic — say so,
and route to strengthening (→ radiology-design / radiology-translation) or a fitting specialty
venue.
