# Submission logistics

Once the ladder is set, get the submission right. Always confirm the target journal's **current**
author instructions (limits change) — verify live (→ radiology-search).

## Article type

- Match the paper to the venue's article types (Original Research / Article / Brief / Technical
  Development). Imaging-AI methodology may fit a technical-development type at some venues.
- Word, abstract, figure, and reference limits differ by type — get them from the current
  instructions, not memory.

## Cover letter (angle, not summary)

- One paragraph: the clinical question, the advance, and the single strongest piece of evidence
  (e.g. external validation result with CI).
- State fit: why *this* journal, briefly.
- Confirm originality, no concurrent submission, ethics approval, and any reporting-checklist
  inclusion (→ radiology-reporting).
- Keep it tight; the editor decides triage from this + abstract.

## Suggested / opposed reviewers

- Suggest methodologically appropriate reviewers (imaging AI + biostatistics + clinical) without
  conflicts of interest.
- Oppose only with a brief, professional reason if needed.

## Required at submission (imaging AI/radiomics)

- The relevant **EQUATOR checklist** (CLAIM/CLEAR/TRIPOD+AI/STARD) — most top venues require it.
- Data/Code Availability statements (→ radiology-data).
- Ethics/consent statement (→ radiology-ethics).

## Transfer cascades

- Some publishers offer transfer to a sister journal on rejection (with reviews). Factor this
  into the ladder: a reach attempt at a cascading family can land softly at a target sibling.

## Turnaround / strategy

- Balance fit, speed, and risk. A long shot at the top tier costs months; weigh against a strong
  target with faster turnaround.

## Pre-flight checklist

```
[ ] Article type + current limits confirmed (live)
[ ] Abstract within format; Summary statement / Key Results if required (→ radiology-writing)
[ ] EQUATOR checklist attached (→ radiology-reporting)
[ ] Data/Code Availability + Ethics statements (→ radiology-data / radiology-ethics)
[ ] Cover letter angled to the venue
[ ] Suggested reviewers (no COI)
[ ] Figures meet spec (→ radiology-figure)
```
