# Discussion — structured

_Radiology_ expects a structured Discussion, not a re-listing of Results.

## Pattern
1. **Paragraph 1 — key findings (concise summary).** Restate, in words + the headline
   numbers, what the study showed. No new data. This paragraph is expected first.
2. **Context / relation to prior work.** How findings agree/disagree with the literature and
   why; what is genuinely added (bounded — avoid "first" unless verifiable).
3. **Mechanism / interpretation.** What the result means clinically/biologically; for
   radiogenomics, bound association vs mechanism.
4. **Limitations.** Honest and specific: retrospective, single-centre, sample size,
   prevalence/spectrum, label noise, scanner generalisability, lack of external validation,
   multiplicity. Pair each limitation with its impact (and any mitigation).
5. **Conclusion / implication.** One or two sentences: what this enables and the next
   validation step. Bounded; no hype; no clinical-deployment claim beyond the evidence.

## Verb calibration
`demonstrate / show` (strong, direct evidence) → `suggest / indicate` (associative) →
`may / could reflect` (speculative). Match the verb to the evidence.

## Rules
- Don't repeat Results figure-by-figure.
- Don't introduce new results or methods.
- Limitations are not a disclaimer dump — they are an honest scope statement; reviewers
  respect specificity.

## Common failures
Over-broad conclusion; "first/novel" unverified; limitations buried or generic; clinical
adoption implied from a single retrospective cohort; mechanism asserted from correlation.
